Taking the First Step as a Career Transition

By Allison Baum

This is a guest post by Escape member Allison Baum. The original post appears here. Allison went to Harvard, worked at Goldman Sachs, then took her own leap out East to lead General Assembly in Hong Kong. We often partner with General Assembly in London and we know that they’re doing great things out in  Hong Kong too – their Web Development Immersive program is a real and very viable option if you’re looking for a way into your own tech startup. Allison blogs here about what she’s learned.

I think it is just beginning to dawn on me how significant of a step I took when I decided to leave my job at Goldman.  I’m a year and half into my P.F. (post-finance) life and, for a variety of reasons, it’s just hitting me now.  

It seems sufficient time has passed such that the public has deemed me beyond the point of no return and they are starting to marvel at my apparent insanity.  I’ve been asked with increasing frequency: “How did you do it? How did you figure out how to leave and not look back?”  Get out there

Expose yourself to your other options in the corporate world by signing up for notifications for job listings.  I had never looked for a job outside finance, I had no idea what was even out there.  There’s a lot of riff raff, but at least you’ll have a sense for your “practical” alternatives and the skills/background they would require.

Join Escape

Sign up for Escape the City.  This was first thing I signed up for after all the traditional newsletters.  Getting exposure to non-traditional options will open your mind and help you consider something you’d never even thought of before.   I didn’t think I’d move to sub-Saharan Africa to work for an NGO, but who knows?  It got me thinking out of the box.
